# Santa Clara County FY2025-26 adopted-budget drill-down

## Bottom line

The defensible countywide chart base is **FY2025-26 adopted net expenditure of $13,043,340,365**, not the headline $13.7 billion gross appropriation. The adopted book reports gross expenditure of **$13,670,954,996**, internal expenditure reimbursements of **-$627,614,631**, net expenditure of **$13,043,340,365**, revenue of **$12,606,174,958**, and net cost/use of fund balance of **$437,165,407**. Net expenditure is the better sunburst denominator because the County says it avoids double-counting reimbursements between budget units in the same fund.

This is an **appropriation/spending-authority view**, not actual cash spending. It should not be labeled "payments" or "tax dollars paid." Percentages below use $13,043,340,365 unless otherwise noted.

Primary sources:

- [FY2025-26 adopted budget book](https://files.santaclaracounty.gov/exjcpb1271/2025-10/fiscal-year-2025-2026-adopted-budget_1.pdf), especially pp. 30-32, "Estimated Revenue and Appropriation for Expenditures by Budget Unit."
- [County FY2025-26 adopted-budget open-data story](https://data.sccgov.org/stories/s/Fiscal-Year-2025-26-Adopted-Budget/bqmh-662y/), which defines appropriation and net expenditure and reports 55% for Health and Hospitals.
- [County budget and finance page](https://www.santaclaracounty.gov/government/budget-and-finance).

Useful level-3 service labels, without defensible adopted dollar splits:

- **SCVH:** four hospitals and their clinics. The County's June 2025 release says the FY25-26 gross growth largely reflects acquisition of Regional Medical Center. Use hospital/clinic labels only as navigation until a reconciled facility-level operating statement is obtained.
- **Behavioral Health:** adult and older adult; children, youth and family; crisis/988/mobile response; substance-use treatment; forensic/diversion/reintegration; contracted community providers. See the [official program directory](https://publichealth.santaclaracounty.gov/programs-and-services) and [department directory](https://publichealth.santaclaracounty.gov/about-us/public-health-department-directory). Do not confuse the separately budgeted Environmental Health unit with Public Health.

### Company financial context (do not blend with County spending)

Motorola Solutions reported **$11.682 billion of worldwide net sales for calendar 2025** in its [2025 Form 10-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/68505/000006850526000010/msi-20251231.htm). That is company revenue from all customers worldwide, **not Santa Clara County payments** and not the denominator for a County tax-dollar chart. The County register's $1,505,832.67 is only the Sheriff's PO value under one agreement.

No broad, current vendor-checkbook dataset was located in the County open-data catalog during this pass. Consequently, the site should display a status badge such as `Budget authority`, `Contract ceiling`, `Encumbered`, or `Paid`, and it should not infer `Paid` from the contract register. The only actual-expenditure fact above is the audit's explicit **$0** for the obsolete South Bay line.

## Caveats and gaps

1. **Budget vs. tax allocation:** County spending is financed by federal/state aid, charges, transfers, bonds and fund balance as well as local taxes. These percentages answer "where the County's adopted net expenditure goes," not "where each property-tax dollar goes" unless a separate revenue-to-expenditure allocation model is built.
2. **Gross vs. net:** Use net expenditure for the sunburst and show gross appropriation in the detail panel. Never mix the two bases within one set of percentages.
3. **Program-level dollars:** Official service directories support labels but not reconciled program amounts. Do not size those service labels from staffing counts, contract ceilings or prior-year cost-center tables.
4. **Hospital facility detail:** A facility-level split for VMC, O'Connor, Saint Louise and Regional Medical Center was not established from the adopted book in this pass. The book's fund table is not automatically equivalent to hospital operating cost and should be reconciled before charting.
5. **Contracts:** Active agreement value is neither amount paid nor necessarily the current-year budget. A contract spanning several years may be amended and may never be fully drawn.
6. **Actuals:** The adopted FY25-26 book was issued during the fiscal year and does not contain full-year FY25-26 actual payments. Add actuals later from the FY25-26 ACFR or a verified County expenditure extract, with transaction date, paying unit, vendor, amount and document identifier.
