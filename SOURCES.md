# Sources and interpretation notes

Last reviewed: 2026-07-21. This is a source inventory for the first release, not a substitute for the citations displayed in the product.

## Tax rules

- Internal Revenue Service, 2025 individual income-tax inflation adjustments and tax tables: [IRS Revenue Procedure 2024-40](https://www.irs.gov/irb/2024-45_IRB). Used for federal brackets, standard deductions, and payroll thresholds.
- California Franchise Tax Board, [2025 Form 540 booklet](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/forms/2025/2025-540-booklet.html). Used for California brackets, standard deductions, credits, and the mental-health surtax threshold.
- California Franchise Tax Board, [residency guidance](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/residency-status/part-year-and-nonresident.html). Used for resident-income context.
- New Jersey Division of Taxation, [employer payroll guidance](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/taxation/businesses/payroll/index.shtml). Used only for the remote-employer warning; it does not calculate New Jersey tax.

## Spending and service context

- Office of Management and Budget, [Historical Table 3.2](https://www.whitehouse.gov/omb/information-resources/budget/historical-tables/). FY2024 actual federal outlays by function and subfunction; $6.735261 trillion common denominator.
- Social Security Administration, [2025 Annual Statistical Supplement highlights](https://www.ssa.gov/policy/docs/statcomps/supplement/2025/highlights.html). Calendar-year 2024 OASI/DI benefit and beneficiary context.
- Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services, [2025 Medicare Trustees Report](https://www.cms.gov/oact/tr/2025). Calendar-year 2024 gross Part A, B, and D program costs. These use a different basis from OMB's net federal outlay.
- U.S. Department of the Treasury, [USAspending Explorer](https://www.usaspending.gov/explorer). Federal award obligations and award-level outlays; individual award records are linked in the product.
- California Department of Finance, [2025–26 enacted budget detail](https://ebudget.ca.gov/budget/e/2025-26/BudgetDetail) and linked enacted department schedules. The root is $321.050481 billion of state-funds budget authority; deeper all-funds views are labeled separately.
- California Open FI$Cal, [department vendor transaction files](https://open.fiscal.ca.gov/). Representative DHCS posted-expenditure records through March 2026; raw and unaudited, not proof of bank settlement.
- County of Santa Clara, [FY2025–26 adopted budget book](https://files.santaclaracounty.gov/exjcpb1271/2025-10/fiscal-year-2025-2026-adopted-budget_1.pdf). County root and budget-unit drill-downs use $13.043340365 billion of adopted net expenditure.
- County of Santa Clara, [active service agreements and Board contracts](https://files.santaclaracounty.gov/exjcpb1596/2026-02/official-sa-bc-report-for-month-of-december-2025_0.pdf) and [Management Audit budget review](https://files.santaclaracounty.gov/exjcpb1666/2025-05/management-audit-division-review-of-fy-2025-26-recommended-budget.pdf). Contract values, encumbrances, and the specifically reported $0 actual line are labeled by status.

## Interpretation and limits

- Budget shares are illustrative allocations of published outlays, not an accounting trace from one taxpayer to one program.
- `Paid/outlay` means the cited system reports a disbursement; `obligated` means legally committed; `budget authority`, `contract value`, and `encumbered` do not prove payment. Open FI$Cal `posted expenditure` is also not treated as proof of bank settlement.
- The county chart is intentionally a budget mix only. Property tax is shared by schools, cities, the county, and special districts, so the app does not claim a personal county-dollar amount.
- The calculator supports basic W-2 scenarios. Self-employment, itemizing, credits beyond qualifying children, capital gains, and city-specific taxes require later models or a tax professional.
- Source data and formulas are versioned; corrections create a new release rather than silently replacing a prior release.
